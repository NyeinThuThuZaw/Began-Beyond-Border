Discover the beauty of Bagan

History


"The Bagan Empire was a powerful kingdom that flourished in Myanmar from the 9th to the 13th centuries. Its zenith was during the 11th and 13th centuries when it became a major center of Buddhist culture and learning. The city of Bagan, located on the banks of the Irrawaddy River, was the capital and the heart of the empire. Bagan is renowned for its vast archaeological site, which features over 2,000 well-preserved pagodas and temples. Despite its fall, Bagan remains a significant historical and cultural landmark, drawing visitors from around the world to explore its ancient ruins and experience the legacy of this once-thriving civilization."



Places

Bagan, Myanmar, is famous for its vast archaeological site featuring thousands of temples and pagodas. Highlights include the Shwezigon Pagoda, Ananda Temple, Dhammayangyi Temple, Sulamani Temple, and Thatbyinnyu Temple. Arts


Bagan is particularly renowned for its architecture, sculpture, and mural paintings, all of which reflect the influence of Buddhism and the artistic traditions of the region.



Architectures


Bagan's architecture, those structures, blending Indian, Mon, and Burmese influences, feature intricate carvings and towering spires.
